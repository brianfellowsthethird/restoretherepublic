We haven't lost our values. We haven't lost our grit. We have lost our balance.

Power drifted upward. Into money. Into politics. Into institutions that no longer answer to the people who built them.

When power concentrates, it always does the same thing. It turns neighbors against each other. It points fingers at race, religion, culture. It keeps us from looking up while the rules are rewritten.

This is not new. It's as old as history.

Power must be limited, divided, and held to account. Markets are strongest when competition is real. Freedom depends on rules applied to everyone. This is not an attack on success. It is a defense of fair play.

Over the next ten years, this agenda would move trillions out of concentrated power and back into the things that make a nation strong: healthcare, education, infrastructure, and opportunity. Lifting the many while ending special treatment for the few.

Those who profit from the current system will fight this hard. They'll call it socialism, communism, or anti-American. That's the lie they always tell.

What truly threatens America is not accountability. It's a nation where a permanent aristocracy lives above the rules. Everyone else is told to fight each other for what's left.

This is about restoring balance. Restoring competition. Restoring the republic.
